Meriwether Lewis

A Man of Undaunted Courage

Here's a timeline of Lewis's Life:

  • 1774 - Born in Virginia
  • 1792 - Takes over operations of family plantation at the age of 18
  • 1794 - Begins Military Career
  • 1801 - Thomas Jefferson's Influence
  • 1801 - 1802 - President Jefferson's personal secretary
  • 1802-1803 - Planning & Preparations
  • 1803 - November '03 Lewis starts down the Ohio River to make winter camp in St. Louis
  • 1804 - Head up the Missouri River, and the Journey Begins
  • 1804 - Winter in Fort Mandan (North Dakota), where they meet Sacagawea
  • 1805 - Crossing the Continental Divide
  • 1805 - November '05 they finally reach the stormy Pacific Ocean (near present-day Astoria, Oregon)
  • 1806 - Begin the journey home in April
  • 1806 - Return to Washington DC
  • 1809 - Lewis dies of gunshot wounds to his stomach at an inn near Nashville. It is unknown if his death was via murder or suicide

"Of courage undaunted, possessing a firmness & perseverance of purpose which nothing but impossibilities could divert from it's direction, careful as a father of those committed to his charge, yet steady in maintenance of order & discipline, intimate with the Indian character, customs & principles, habituated to the hunting life, guarded by exact ovbersavtion of the vegetables & animals of his own country, against losing time in the description of objects already possessed, honest, disinterested, liberal, of sound understanding and a fidelity to truth so scrupulous that whatever he should report would be as certain as to have seen it ourselves, with all these qualifications as if selected and implanted by nature in one body, for this express purpose, I could have no hesitation in confiding the enterprise to him."

-- Thomas Jefferson on Meriwether Lewis
